Your Learning Outcomes

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

  1. Define and describe the Core Values model of family recovery coaching
  2. Distinguish family coaching from intervention, executive coaching, therapy, sponsorship, and peer roles
  3. Apply the Circle of Control to empower families toward healthy choices
  4. Facilitate the ten core modules within the 52-week family curriculum
  5. Demonstrate ethical alignment with the Core Values Code of Ethics
  6. Use Core Values language (Healthy Choices, Pivot Person, etc.) with precision
  7. Navigate the three stages of family recovery: Landing, Stabilization, Optimization
  8. Identify and reframe codependency patterns without shaming clients
  9. Collaborate with the Core Values ecosystem using shared language and boundaries
  10. Prepare for the 40-week mentorship with clarity on professional expectations

40-Week Mentorship Model

Sustaining growth long after the three-day intensive

Purpose of Mentorship

Mentorship ensures you receive ongoing support while delivering the 52-week family recovery program to real clients. Use this space to map out your mentorship journey.

Mentorship Phases

Months 1-3

Weekly check-ins focused on early sessions, supervision, and ethical alignment.

  • Review first client sessions
  • Practice module delivery with feedback
  • Address ethical dilemmas and boundaries

Months 4-6

Bi-weekly check-ins emphasizing advanced modules, collaboration, and style.

  • Case consultation for active clients
  • Deepen work with challenging modules
  • Integrate with the broader Core Values team

Months 7-10

Monthly check-ins focused on mastery, leadership, and certification readiness.

  • Advanced case consultation
  • Professional development planning
  • Master Coach application preparation

Mentorship Requirements

  • Minimum of three active family coaching clients during mentorship
  • Deliver all ten modules with at least one family
  • Document sessions and submit case reflections
  • Participate in peer consultation groups
  • Demonstrate ethical practice and professional boundaries
